# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

D2C ecommerce founders and marketers (typically $1M-$50M revenue Shopify brands) evaluating whether to invest in CRO. They arrive from search, referrals, or Jono's outreach. Context: skeptical of agency promises, numerate, short on time. The free tools (calculators) also pull in practitioners searching for a quick answer (RPV, A/B significance, shipping thresholds) who become leads.

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for Impact Conversion, a research-led CRO consultancy run by Jono Matla. The site's job is to demonstrate the methodology (research first, tests called with statistical rigour) and convert visitors into booked 15-minute consults. Free tools earn search traffic and demonstrate competence by being genuinely useful. Success = qualified consult bookings.

## Brand Personality

Direct, evidence-based, no fluff. Talks like a practitioner, not an agency. Confident enough to show the maths. Warm but never salesy.

## Anti-references

- Corporate agency jargon (synergy, holistic, best-in-class) and marketing buzzwords.
- Fake or unsourced stats. Every number must trace to a real source or the user's own inputs.
- Generic SaaS-template design: cookie-cutter card grids, gradient text, hero-metric clichés.
- Em dashes in copy. Never.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what we preach**: a CRO consultancy's site must itself be a conversion-craft exhibit. Every page earns its next scroll.
2. **Show the maths**: calculators, forecasts, and claims expose their assumptions instead of hiding them.
3. **Useful before persuasive**: free tools solve the visitor's problem fully, then offer the consult.
4. **One accent, used sparingly**: the purple carries actions and key results; everything else stays quiet.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard-visible focus rings (already global), prefers-reduced-motion alternatives for all animation, form inputs with real labels, contrast ≥4.5:1 for body text.
