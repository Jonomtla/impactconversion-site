# Homepage hero rubric — IC site

**Surface:** `components/Hero.tsx` — H1 + subhead. The single most-seen block on the site. Every visitor reads it first.
**Job:** Make a $5M-$20M D2C or online-ed marketing leader (a) recognise the problem in their language within 3 seconds, (b) believe the lever named (research-led testing on existing traffic) is the right answer, (c) want to keep reading.

Pass threshold: **13/15** (homepage hero is higher-stakes than card summaries — tighter bar).

Length budget:
- H1: 8-14 words
- Subhead: 25-50 words

---

## H1 items (7)

| # | Item | What it tests |
|---|---|---|
| H1-1 | ≤14 words | Hero scannability |
| H1-2 | Names something concrete the prospect already pays for or owns (traffic, ads, site, funnel, conversion, revenue) | CRE problem-anchoring; Schwartz market-aware |
| H1-3 | Verb in the sentence (not pure noun headline) | Wilbers Action |
| H1-4 | Either antithetical (`X but Y`), periodic (build-then-payoff), or balanced — not flat declarative | Wilbers Music |
| H1-5 | Implicit or explicit `you` viewpoint — prospect can place themselves | Wilbers POV; Halbert/Schwartz |
| H1-6 | No hollow intensifiers, no nominalizations, no em dashes | Wilbers Economy/Action; house |
| H1-7 | Spoiler beats teaser — names the payoff or the pain, not a riddle | DR direct-response doctrine |

## Subhead items (8)

| # | Item | What it tests |
|---|---|---|
| S-1 | 25-50 words | Length budget |
| S-2 | At least one sentence ≤10 words AND at least one ≥18 words | Wilbers Music — variety |
| S-3 | Names the lever (research, testing, A/B, experimentation, conversion) — what IC actually does | CRE method-anchoring |
| S-4 | Names a CRE-doctrine outcome (compounding, revenue per visitor, same ad spend, lifetime value, AOV) | Schwartz prospect-direct; CRE |
| S-5 | Active voice throughout (passive only if actor genuinely irrelevant) | Wilbers Action |
| S-6 | No hollow intensifiers, no nominalizations | Wilbers Economy |
| S-7 | Sentence-to-sentence coherence — old-info-first, new-info-second | Wilbers Coherence |
| S-8 | No em dashes, no fragments stacked as default rhythm (max 1 fragment, only as earned closing emphasis) | Wilbers Music + house |

---

## Tiebreaker order

1. Higher H1-Music score (H1-4) — variety is the recurring IC gap
2. Stronger H1-2 anchor — named noun the prospect can verify they own
3. Higher S-2 + S-3 score — lever named with rhythmic variety
4. Read-aloud test — the one Jono would say in a sales call
